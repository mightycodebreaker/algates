Your Guide to Term Insurance

What is the most important thing for you in life? Ofcourse, your family, the people you love the most.

What can you possibly do to protect your family in case of any emergency or unfortunate event which puts your family in financial troubles?

Ofcourse, you would say that you would go to any extent to protect your family from any such trouble.

So have you thought about buying a term insurance plan for yourself?

This term insurance plan will help your family sail through any financial trouble in case of the unfortunate event of your passing on.

We will tell you how.

What is Term Insurance?

Term Life Insurance is a pure protection plan which gives your family a huge amount of money in case you pass on any time during the term of the policy.

This huge amount of money which is received by your family in such a case is called Sum Assured. You can also call this Cover Amount. In return, you have to pay a small amount to the insurance company every year. This is called Premium.

In case you survive the entire term of the policy, your term insurance plan does not pay you anything. That is why it is a pure protection plan and the premiums which you pay are very small.

This term insurance plan is a legal contract between you and your insurance company. Once you purchase a term insurance plan, your life insurance company sends you a hard copy and a soft copy of this legal contract. This is your policy document.

This policy document contains all the terms and conditions of this contract.

Right hand side will be an illustration of an excited person or happy customer.

What should be your cover amount?

Think of it this way.

What do you want your term insurance policy in your absence? Do you want it to replace your income so that your family continues to have the same amount of money every month as they used to have when you were around?.

Or do you want it to replace your expenses so that your dependents can continue to have the same lifestyle which they were used to even in your absence.

Or do you want it to cover your liabilities such as home loan or any other loan in case you pass on? After all, you are trying to provide financial security for your loved ones in your absence.

As a rule of thumb, you can opt for a sum assured of 15 to 20 times of your annual income. In case you have any liabilities, such as a home loan, you can add the outstanding amount to this.

So if you are a 30 year old and your annual income is INR 10 lacs, you can opt for a sum assured of INR 2 crores which would be 20 times of your annual income. If you are 50 years old with the same income, you can opt for only 10 times of your annual income i.e. INR 1 crore.

If you wish to calculate the ideal sum assured for yourself, you can check out our term plan sum assured calculator.

Financial security to family against uncertainties.

If you are the sole breadwinner for your family, your loved ones completely depend on monthly income for living and maintaining a lifestyle.

Now imagine if you are not around to bring that income home every month, your loved one would not be able to maintain the lifestyle they were used to. They might have to face great financial hardships as well.

This is the time when a term insurance plan can help your family and provide them financial security.

In case of the unfortunate event of your passing on, term insurance plan provides a huge sum of money to your family. This helps your family sail through the financial hardships when you are not around.

Safeguard against liabilities

You might have taken a loan to buy a home or car. These loans are usually paid off over a number of years in the form of EMIs.

In case you pass on during this period, your family will be left behind with the burden of paying the outstanding loan.

If you already have term insurance in place, the amount your family gets from the term insurance can help them to pay off such loans.

Nuclear families benefit a lot.

Over the last 2 to 3 decades, there is a social and cultural shift in India from joint families to nuclear families. Social security and support provided informally by joint families is lost.

Most likely, you are the only financial support system for your family. If you don’t buy a term insurance plan, your absence can make life very difficult for your family.

Buy a term insurance plan and provide your family the best financial security you can.

Cover your Elderly Parents

When you are young and have just started earning, even if you are not married and don’t have kids, your parents might be depending on you for long term care.

Buy a term insurance plan early so that you can cover your parents in case you pass on.

We always recommend you to buy a term insurance plan soon after you start earning.

Low Premiums

Unlike any other life insurance plan, you can buy a term insurance plan very cheap.

For example, assume that you are a 27 year old and you are looking for a term insurance plan for yourself with a sum assured of Rs. 50 lakhs sum, you can get it for as low Rs. 4000 which you need to pay annually to the insurance company.

The premium amount which you need to pay increases as you grow older and increases faster once you cross the age of 35 years.

Buy a term plan for yourself early to lock in low premiums for life and save money.

Tax Benefit

Buy a term insurance policy and get tax benefits as well.

The premiums you pay to the insurance company are exempted from income tax under section 80(C) of the Income Tax Act.

What is NOT covered in Term Insurance?

It is always good for you to know the conditions under which your insurance company might NOT settle your death claim. Yes, you read it right. There are a few caveats attached.

Suicides are horrible and can be the cause huge distress for loved ones who are left to deal with your sudden departure. Yet, term insurance plan usually does not provide you cover if you die of suicide during the first year of your policy.

Waiver of Premium (WOP)

Imagine you have a term insurance policy and you have kept the policy running by paying regular premiums for last few years. Now you fall sick and this sickness is so severe that you get confined to a wheelchair for sometime. I know it’s a nasty thing to say but you know this can happen to anyone.

So just imagine if this happens to you, you would have a lot of other things to worry about. You might lose your day job because you could no longer go to the office.

You would have to pay for monthly rent, utilities, groceries and to top it all, you would also have to pay for your treatment. Paying Rs. 10 to 15 thousand to keep your term insurance policy running would be the last thing you would want to do.

In this situation, you can ask your insurance company to keep your term insurance policy running by paying premiums on your behalf. All you have to do to activate this option is buy this add-on called Waiver of Premium by paying a little extra premium.

Insurance companies usually offer Waiver of Premium of Option in case of disability or critical illness such as Cancer. Please check what your insurance company offers before deciding on buying this add-on.

Accidental Death Benefit (ADB)

Life is full of uncertainties. Accidents happen which snatch peoples’ lives.

This add-on provides extra benefit to your family in case you die in an accident. Just to give you a perspective, imagine you bought a term insurance policy with a cover of Rs. 1 crore. You also took an accidental death benefit add-on with a cover of Rs. 1 crore.

In such a scenario, in case you die in an accident, your family will get Rs. 2 crores from the insurance company. This add-on gives an extra cushion just in case of accidental death and it comes very cheap. So why not have it, right?

But remember, in case you die of any other reason other than accident, your family just gets the basic sum assured of Rs. 1 crore. So it is not a good idea to reduce your basic term insurance cover amount just because you opted for this add-on.

Critical Illness

Critical illnesses are difficult to deal with. God forbid, if you get any critical illness such as Cancer, it will take you a long time to undergo the treatment.

More likely, you will have to take a break from your paid job to undergo the treatment for a year or two. This results in you losing your regular income for sometime. Returning to a regular job after a long break can also be difficult.

Treatment of any critical illness also drains you and your family financially as it is very expensive. Imagine, what would be the situation if this were to happen 5 years from now. Steep rise in cost of medical treatment will make the situation worse.

We recommend you to buy a Critical Illness add-on which will help you deal with such situations. Critical illness add-on will give you the cover amount when your doctor says that you have got any critical illness. Remember, that critical illness should be included in the add-on you buy.

You can use this cover amount to get yourself treated or to cover your other expenses.

This add-on is usually expensive and gets more expensive as you grow older. Rates also depend on the number of critical illnesses that are covered. We recommend you to read the details before you decide to purchase this.

Terminal Illness

Terminal illness is any illness in which your doctor declares that there is no treatment which can be done to save you and you are surely going to die in the coming 180 days. This is the last thing you would want.
