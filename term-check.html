Your Checklist for Term Insurance

What you need to check?

Insurance plans are rigid in nature. Once you buy a term insurance plan, you make a long term commitment to agree to all its terms and conditions.

Imagine that you bought a term insurance plan for yourself today and after a year from now, when you are about to pay the next premium installment, you realise that this policy does not provide you enough cover.

Now, can you increase your cover amount in that policy? Most likely, you will not be allowed to do that. Then what do you do?

Continue with this policy and buy another policy for an extra cover amount?

Or discontinue this policy and buy another policy with a bigger cover amount?

In both cases, you have to go through the hassle of buying an extra policy and maintaining it. Why get into such a situation?

Instead, make sure you have these basics right before you make your first term insurance purchase.

Need for term insurance policy

Do your family members depend on your regular income for their financial needs? Do you have small kids whom you have to raise? Do you have elderly parents to take care of?

If yes, you need a term insurance plan. This term insurance plan will help them sail through the difficult times in case you are no more.

Right time to buy term insurance

Most people, even after realising that they need a term insurance plan, tend to postpone buying it until it is too late.

You see, buying a term insurance policy has its own benefits. You get the cover early and also get the benefit of locking lower premiums for life.

Also, as you grow old, many lifestyle illnesses tend to set in such as obesity, hypertension, diabetes etc. At 25, you are young and healthy. At 35, you age and look a bit older. At 45, you already have some illness for which you take regular medication. This is an example just to give you a perspective.

Life insurance companies prefer to provide cover to you when you are 25. At 35, they will charge a higher premium for the same cover as you have aged. At 45, they will charge an even higher premium. At this time, they might even decline to give you cover since you have already been on medication for some time.

Many people are shocked to know this. We always recommend you to buy a term insurance policy when you are young and healthy.

Adequate Cover Amount

You are not going to change your term insurance policy frequently. Also, you will not be able to make changes in the existing one.

So buy one insurance policy which provides you adequate cover for your age and income.

For how long should you buy your term insurance policy?

At what age do you think you would retire from your regular job? At what age do you think your kids will become financially independent? At 60?.

Then your term insurance policy should provide you cover till you get 60 years old. You can always take a policy which provides you cover for a longer time, but that will be more expensive.

Do you need any add-ons?

You get various add-ons with your basis term insurance plan. Add-ons come at an extra cost.

Please consider them well before buying your policy. You will not be able to add them after you have already bought you term plan.
